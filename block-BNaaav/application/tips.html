Tips

If it's your first time being a plant parent, go through our tips below to make sure your new plant stays fresh & healthy!

Plant Care

  • Plants should be pruned occasionally to maintain their shape, to remove unwanted damaged parts and to promote new fresh growth.
  • Regular checks should be done for any sign of infection by plant pests.
  • To avoid growth of plants in one direction (the direction from which it gets light), turn the plant occasionally to promote fuller growth in all directions.
  • Smaller plants Are the fastest growing plants

Light

  • Generally, ornamental plants don't require a lot of sunlight, whereas succulents needs a lot of it. Flowering plants need access to direct sunlight.
  • Many plants, especially flowering types grow at their best close to a window in partial sun and shade.
  • Flowers not blooming and yellowing leaves are an indication of a plant not receiving enough sunlight.
  • Drooping leaves and leaves shrivelling up are indicators of a plant receiving too much sunlight.

Water

  • Water deeply, rather than lightly and frequently
